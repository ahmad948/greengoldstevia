What is steviaðŸŒ¿?

Stevia is a natural sweetener derived from the leaves of the Stevia rebaudiana plant. It's 200-300 times sweeter than sugar, yet has a negligible effect on blood sugar levels, making it an ideal choice for sugar patients and those managing diabetes. Stevia is calorie-free, non-glycemic, and has been used for centuries in traditional medicine.

how stevia is made?

Step 1: Harvesting

Stevia leaves are carefully handpicked at their peak to ensure the best quality.

Step 2: Drying

The leaves are dried under the sun or using gentle heat, locking in their natural sweetness.

Step 3: Extraction

The extract is then purified to remove any impurities, ensuring only the purest form of sweetness.

Step 4 Purification

The dried leaves undergo a water-based extraction process, separating the sweet compounds, mainly steviosides and rebaudiosides.

Step 5: Crystallization

The purified extract is crystallized, transforming into the fine powder you know and love.

FAQs

Why is there only a small percentage of stevia in Splenda Stevia?

Steviol glycoside is 200 times sweeter than sugar. To create a usable product with a sweetness level similar to sugar the stevia is diluted with erythritol. The erythritol ads more volume to the stevia so you can replace a spoon sugar by a spoon Splenda Stevia.

Are Splenda Sweeteners suitable for use by people with diabetes?

Splenda Sweetener Products are suitable for people with diabetes as part of their healthy everyday diet. Splenda Sweeteners contain sucralose which is a calorie free and carbohydrate free sweetener.

What does stevia do to the body?

Using stevia as a sugar substitute can reduce calories in foods and drinks, but it may also have some adverse effects. Possible side effects include nausea, bloating, low blood pressure, and hormone disruption. Stevia is a non-nutritive or zero-calorie sweetener made of steviol glycosides.
